FAQs

What documents do I need to rent a car?

To rent a car, you typically need:

  • A valid driver's license (must be held for at least one year)
  • A credit card in the name of the primary driver
  • Proof of insurance (if you opt not to purchase rental insurance)
  • An international driving permit (if renting outside your home country)
What is the minimum age to rent a car?

The minimum age requirement varies by location and rental company, but typically ranges from 21-25 years old. Additional fees may apply for drivers under 25.

Can I rent a car with a debit card?

While some rental companies accept debit cards, many prefer or require credit cards. Additional documentation and a larger deposit may be required when using a debit card.

What is the fuel policy for rentals?

Most rental companies provide the car with a full tank and expect it to be returned full. If not returned full, you may be charged a premium rate for the missing fuel.

What happens if I return the car late?

Late returns may incur additional charges. Most companies have a grace period of 29-59 minutes, after which you may be charged for an extra day.
